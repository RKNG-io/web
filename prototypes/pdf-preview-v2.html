Phase 1

Foundation ‚Äî This Week

These are the non-negotiables. The legal and financial basics that let you take money without worry.

‚öñÔ∏è Legal
Review your employment contract
Look for non-compete clauses, moonlighting restrictions, IP assignment, disclosure requirements.
Choose your business structure
Sole trader (simplest) or limited company (separate legal entity). Most start as sole traders.
‚Üí gov.uk/set-up-business
Check if you need licenses or permits
Coaching: usually none. Financial advice: yes. Check to be sure.
‚Üí gov.uk/licence-finder
Draft a simple client agreement
What you'll deliver, what they'll pay, cancellation terms. A clear email can work to start.
üìã Compliance
Register with HMRC for Self Assessment
Required if you'll earn over ¬£1,000/year. You'll receive a UTR within 10 working days.
‚Üí gov.uk/register-for-self-assessment
Set up basic record-keeping
Keep invoices, receipts, bank statements for 5 years. A spreadsheet works.
Check GDPR requirements
If you collect client data: you need a privacy policy. ICO registration: ¬£40/year.
‚Üí ico.org.uk/for-small-organisations

üí∞ Finance
Open a business bank account
Not legally required for sole traders, but makes tax return much easier. Many offer free first year.

Phase 2

Operations ‚Äî Weeks 2-4

The systems that let you work with clients smoothly. Build once, use forever.

‚öôÔ∏è Operations
Set up online booking
Calendly (free tier), Cal.com, or Acuity. Link payment to booking so clients pay when they book.
Create client intake process
What info do you need before the first session? Questionnaire, contract, payment ‚Äî automate the sequence.
Document your service delivery process
What's your framework? Write it down. Makes delivery consistent and takes thinking out of each session.
Set up invoicing
If not using payment-at-booking. Options: Wave (free), Xero, QuickBooks.
Create a simple client tracker
Even a spreadsheet: Name, contact, sessions booked, notes. Know who you're working with.
üåê Presence

‚Üí google.com/business
Set up professional email
sarah@sarahchencoaching.com looks better than a gmail address.

Growth ‚Äî Month 2+

You're set up. Now it's time to get visible and build momentum.

üì£ Marketing
Tell your network you're open for business
LinkedIn post. Personal emails to warm contacts. Your first clients come from people who already know you.
