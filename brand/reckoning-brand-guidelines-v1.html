01 — Foundation

Brand Essence

Reckoning helps solopreneurs and small business owners stop dreaming and start running. It's the bridge between the life you're living and the one you've been waiting to build.

The Name

"Reckoning" is a moment of truth — when accounts are settled, clarity emerges, and decisions become real. It carries weight without heaviness, urgency without panic. It's both an assessment and a beginning.

Brand Promise

We meet you where you are with clear-eyed diagnosis and prioritised action. No jargon, no upsells, no busywork. Just the next right step, delivered by people who understand systems.

Tagline

Your time is now.

Brand Pillars

Clarity

Cut through the noise. Know exactly where you stand and what to do next.

Action

No more planning paralysis. Prioritised steps you can take today.

Support

You're not alone. Expert help, transparently priced, when you need it.

02 — Foundation

Brand Personality

Reckoning feels like a trusted friend who's been where you're going — confident without ego, direct without coldness, energetic without chaos.

We are

Fresh · Optimistic · Approachable · Direct · Warm · Energetic · Supportive

We are not

Corporate · Pushy · Jargon-heavy · Slick · Chaotic · Cold · Intimidating

Brand Tension

We hold two truths at once: This is your moment — and you don't have to figure it out alone. The visual identity balances energy with calm, confidence with approachability, boldness with support.

Emotional Journey

Before

Overwhelmed, uncertain, stuck in planning mode, doing everything alone.

During

Clarity emerging, priorities becoming clear, feeling seen and understood.

After

Confident, focused, supported, moving forward with clear next steps.

03 — Foundation

Voice & Tone

We speak like a smart friend who happens to understand business operations — clear, direct, encouraging, never condescending.

Voice Principles

Clear over clever

Say what you mean. No buzzwords, no hedging, no unnecessary complexity.

Warm, not soft

Kindness and directness aren't opposites. We care enough to be honest.

Grounded optimism

We believe in possibility without ignoring reality. Hope with a plan.

Tone by Context

Context Tone Example
Marketing / Home Energetic, inviting "Your time is now. Let's make it count."
Questionnaire Warm, conversational "Tell us about your business — no wrong answers."
Diagnosis / Report Direct, supportive "Here's what's costing you. Here's what to do about it."
Error / Empty state Calm, helpful "Something went wrong. Let's try that again."

Examples

Do
"You're doing three people's jobs. Let's fix that."
Don't
"Leverage our synergistic solutions to optimise your workflow efficiency."
Do
"Ready when you are. No pressure."
Don't
"Act now! Limited time offer! Don't miss out!"
Do
"This will take about 15 minutes. Worth it."
Don't
"Complete our comprehensive diagnostic assessment tool."

10 — Design System

Colour Usage

Three accents need clear hierarchy. Use them intentionally — not all at once.

Colour Roles

Colour Role Use For Don't Use For
Fuchsia Action / Energy Primary CTAs, rising line, links, key moments Large backgrounds, body text
Mint Fresh / Optimism Section backgrounds, highlights, success states, cards CTAs, text, critical actions
Blue Calm / Balance Secondary buttons, borders, disabled states, subtle backgrounds Primary actions, headlines
Charcoal Foundation Text, headers, dark sections, secondary buttons —
Ice Canvas Page backgrounds, cards, light sections Text, buttons

Ratio Guidelines

Typical Page

70% Charcoal + Ice (foundation)

15% Mint or Blue (sections)

15% Fuchsia (accents only)

Hero Section

80% Charcoal background

15% White text

5% Fuchsia CTA + accent

Feature Section

60% Mint background

35% Charcoal text

5% Fuchsia accents

Avoid

Don't use all three accents in the same section

Don't use fuchsia for large background areas

Don't place mint and blue directly adjacent

15 — Application

Accessibility

Ensure the brand is usable by everyone. Follow WCAG 2.1 AA guidelines as a minimum.

Colour Contrast

Combination Ratio WCAG AA Use For
Charcoal on Ice 12.5:1 ✓ Pass Body text, headers
White on Charcoal 12.5:1 ✓ Pass Body text, headers
White on Fuchsia 4.6:1 ✓ Pass Button text (large)
Fuchsia on Ice 4.1:1 ~ Large text only Links, large text, icons
Charcoal on Mint 8.2:1 ✓ Pass Text on mint sections
Charcoal on Blue 6.8:1 ✓ Pass Text on blue sections

Guidelines

Do

  • Use Charcoal for body text
  • Ensure interactive elements have visible focus states
  • Provide text alternatives for images
  • Test with screen readers

Don't

  • Use Fuchsia for small body text
  • Rely on colour alone to convey information
  • Use Mint or Blue for text
  • Disable zoom or text scaling
