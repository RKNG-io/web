1. Positioning

We find what's costing you time.

Most business owners lose 10+ hours a week to tasks that could run themselves. We spot them. Then we fix them.

What Reckoning Actually Is

Automations for people who don't know about automations.

The diagnostic reveals what's possible. The services deliver it.

The One-Liner Test

If someone asks "what does Reckoning do?", the answer is:

"We look at how you spend your week and show you what could run itself. Then we build it for you."

What We're Not

  • Not a coaching programme
  • Not a course
  • Not general business consulting
  • Not "helping you find your purpose"

We find time leaks. We plug them with automations. That's it.

Who It's For

Small business owners and solopreneurs who:

  • Are doing tasks they suspect could be automated
  • Don't know what's possible
  • Don't have time to figure it out
  • Would pay someone to just do it

Voice (Unchanged from v1)

We are

Direct · Warm · Clear · Optimistic · Supportive

We are not

Salesy · Jargon-heavy · Pushy · Cold · Vague

2. User Journey

Landing Page → Intake (5 min) → Diagnostic → Services (optional)

The Core Loop

  1. Land — "We find what's costing you time"
  2. Intake — Tell us about your week (5 minutes, max)
  3. Diagnostic — Here's 3 things we can automate + what each saves you
  4. Choose — Pick one, we'll build it (or run with it yourself)

No Personas

v1 had launcher/builder/architect. v2 doesn't segment. Everyone gets the same intake. The diagnostic adapts based on answers, not pre-assigned labels.

Two Paths to Value

Run With It

Free diagnostic. You see what's possible. Take it from there.

Done-for-You

Same diagnostic. We build it. Transparent pricing. No calls required.

Both paths are valid. We show them what's possible — that's the value. If they want help building it, we're here.

Headline Options

  • "Your clients book, pay, and show up — automatically."

Headline Options

  3. Last-minute cancellations — Empty slots, lost revenue

Example Automations

  • Appointment ends → "Book your next session" email with link
  • Digital intake form → saved to client record → pre-filled next time
  • Cancellation → waitlist notification → slot filled automatically
  • 6 weeks since last visit → "Time for your next [treatment]?" reminder
Vertical 3

Trades

4. The Intake

5 minutes. Focused on time costs. No psychological deep-dive.

Design Principles

  • One question per screen
  • Progress indicator (not percentage — stages)
  • Can go back
  • Saves progress (localStorage)
  • Email captured at the end, not the start

Question Flow

Screen 1: Name

"What should we call you?"

Text input. Required.

Screen 2: Business

"What's your business? (A few words is fine.)"

Text input. Examples shown: "Life coaching", "Freelance design", "Online bakery"

Screen 3: Stage

"Where are you right now?"

  • Just getting started (no customers yet)
  • Got some customers, building momentum
  • Established, looking to scale or streamline

Single select.

Screen 4: Hours

"How many hours a week do you spend on your business?"

Number input or slider (5–60+ range).

Screen 5: Time Sinks

"What eats the most time? (Pick up to 3)"

  • Emails and messages
  • Invoicing and chasing payments
  • Scheduling and calendar
  • Social media
  • Admin and data entry
  • Client onboarding
  • Creating content
  • Something else → [text field]

Multi-select, max 3.

Screen 6: Biggest Frustration

"What's the one thing you wish just... ran itself?"

Text input. This is gold for the diagnostic.

Screen 7: Tools

"What tools do you already use? (Select any that apply)"

  • Google Workspace
  • Microsoft 365
  • Xero / QuickBooks
  • Calendly / Acuity
  • Stripe / PayPal
  • Mailchimp / ConvertKit
  • Notion / Trello / Asana
  • Instagram / Facebook / LinkedIn
  • Other → [text field]

Multi-select.

Screen 8: Email

"Where should we send your diagnostic?"

Email input. Required.

Checkbox: "Send me occasional tips on saving time (you can unsubscribe anytime)"

Screen 9: Processing

"Building your diagnostic..."

Loading state. ~30 seconds. Then redirect to diagnostic page.

Micro-Copy

  • Acknowledge answers briefly: "Got it.", "That's helpful.", "Noted."
  • No cheery exclamation marks
  • No "Great choice!"

5. The Diagnostic

One page. Three automations. Clear value. No fluff.

Structure

Header

[Name], here's what we found.

You're spending roughly [X] hours a week on [top time sink]. Here's how to get some of that back.

Section 1: The Opportunities

Three cards. Each contains:

  • What — The automation (e.g., "Automated invoice reminders")
  • Time saved — Hours per week (e.g., "~2 hrs/week")
  • How it works — One sentence (e.g., "Xero sends reminders at 7, 14, 21 days automatically")
  • Done-for-you — Price + "Get this" button

Note: We show what's possible. No links, no step-by-step instructions. They leave knowing what could change — and we're here if they want help making it happen.

Section 2: The Maths

Simple calculation:

[Hours saved/week] × [Weeks/year] = [Hours back/year]

At £[their implied hourly rate], that's £[annual value] in time.

Only show if we have enough data to make it credible. Otherwise, skip.

Section 3: Your Next Step

One highlighted action:

"If you do one thing this week: [specific action]"

Service CTA or "Run with it yourself — we're here when you need us."

Footer

  • Download as PDF (one-pager)
  • Share link (public URL with token)
  • "Questions? Reply to the email — it goes to a human."

7. Agent Architecture

Internal system. Four components. Customer never sees this.

Note: This is how the sausage gets made. None of this is customer-facing. They just see the diagnostic.

Agent 1

Intake Processor

Receives questionnaire submission. Structures data. Passes to Diagnostic Generator.

Input: Raw questionnaire answers
Output: Structured intake object

Agent 2

Diagnostic Generator

Analyses intake. Identifies automation opportunities. Calculates time savings. Generates diagnostic JSON.

Input: Structured intake
Output: Diagnostic JSON (see schema above)

Agent 3

Humaniser

Takes diagnostic JSON. Writes the copy. Ensures tone is warm, direct, on-brand. Uses their exact words where possible.

Input: Diagnostic JSON + intake answers
Output: Humanised diagnostic ready for display

Agent 4

Validator

Checks output for: accuracy (maths), brand voice (no banned phrases), personalisation (uses their name/words), service validity (IDs exist).

Input: Humanised diagnostic
Output: Confidence score + flags

Threshold: ≥90% auto-send. <90% queue for review.

Flow

Intake → Processor → Generator → Humaniser → Validator → [Auto-send or Review Queue]

Validation Rules (Carried from v1)

  • Name match — Diagnostic must use their submitted name
  • Maths check — Calculations must be accurate
  • Service IDs — Must exist in service catalogue
  • Banned phrases — "you should", "you must", "most businesses fail", "leverage", "synergy"
  • Quoted phrases — Must include at least one phrase from their answers

10. Mobile-First Design

Design for mobile first. Desktop is the enhancement, not the default.

Why Mobile-First Matters Here

  • Fitness — PTs are on their phones between sessions
  • Wellness — Therapists check emails on breaks, rarely at a desk
  • Trades — In the van, on site, phone is the only screen
  • Events — Running around venues, phone is their office

These audiences live on mobile. If it doesn't work on a phone, it doesn't work.

Design Constraints

Intake

  • One question per screen — no scrolling to find the "Next" button
  • Large tap targets (min 48px height)
  • Thumb-friendly button placement (bottom of screen)
  • Progress indicator always visible
  • No horizontal scrolling ever

Diagnostic

  • Cards stack vertically
  • Expandable sections for detail (not all visible at once)
  • Sticky CTA at bottom of viewport
  • PDF download works on mobile (not just "open in browser")

Landing Pages

  • Hero text readable without zooming
  • Single-column layout below 768px
  • Images optimised (WebP, lazy loading)
  • No hover-dependent interactions

Navigation

  • Hamburger menu on mobile (not visible nav items)
  • Logo links home
  • Primary CTA always accessible (sticky or in hamburger)

Breakpoints

Breakpoint Target Notes
< 640px Mobile Primary design target
640px – 1024px Tablet 2-column where appropriate
> 1024px Desktop Full layout, max-width container

Testing Requirement

Every page must be tested on:

  • iPhone SE (small screen)
  • iPhone 14 Pro (standard)
  • Android mid-range (e.g., Samsung A series)

If it breaks on iPhone SE, it's not done.
